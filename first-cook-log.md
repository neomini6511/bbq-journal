## Ingredients and prep notes
- Beef rib, about 1 kg, from Fleisch Discounter
- Binder: 50% vinegar, 50% mustard
- Seasoning: 50% salt, 50% pepper, with salt applied first and pepper second
- Spritz during cook: water and vinegar
- Potatoes: 0.8 kg, cut small, washed, placed in warm water with salt, boiled 5 to 8 minutes, then planned for air frying in the grill
- Potato seasoning mix: 2 spoons country cuts seasoning, 2 spoons sticky rice powder, 1 spoon oil
- Grill: Ninja Woodfire XL
- Fuel: pellets

## Outcome and tasting notes
- The beef was excellent.
- Overall rating: 8.5 out of 10.
- Portion size was enough for two people.
- Overall outcome made both diners quite happy.
- Beef exterior / bark was quite salty and very dark at the end, but the taste was still really good.
- It was noted that the beef did not look as dark at the time of wrapping or when it first came out, but after resting for about one hour it became much darker.
- Potatoes were not ideal.
- The potato mix with sticky rice powder was not perfect.
- Potatoes were cooked too long, although they still had crunch.
- Next time, try a different potato coating or seasoning approach.

## Lessons / next-time notes
- Be careful when probing through foil. Avoid poking holes in the aluminum foil, because this time a hole was made and a lot of oil / juices appeared to leak through.
- Watch salt level on the beef bark, because the exterior ended up quite salty.
- Watch final darkening after rest, because the beef became noticeably darker during the one-hour foil rest.
- Rework the potato coating. The sticky rice powder approach was not ideal.
- Consider a shorter potato cook time next time.

## Open questions / details to capture later
